Writing the history of the Festival d'Avignon through its digital traces

What can the Festival d’Avignon’s still largely unexplored digital archives reveal? As digital records continue to grow, they raise new questions about how we collect, preserve, and study the history of contemporary performance.

Since its founding in 1947, the Festival d’Avignon has generated an exceptional body of archival material. Over the past three decades, an increasing share of these records has been created and preserved in digital form—from emails and websites to social media, video recordings, photographs, and databases. Far from simply transforming archival practices, these digital traces are reshaping the ways we document, interpret, and write the cultural history of the performing arts.

Organized by the ERC From Stage to Data project, this conference is open to all and brings together researchers, archivists, museum and cultural heritage professionals, artists, and representatives of cultural institutions. The event will be conducted primarily, though not exclusively, in French.

Program

Saturday, 11 July
9:00 am Welcome
9:30 am The Digital Taste of the Archive Roundtable
How are born-digital archives already shaping the memory of contemporary theater? Archivists and representatives of cultural institutions discuss what should be preserved, transmitted, and made accessible.
With Juliette Caron (Odéon–Théâtre de l'Europe), Lou Forster (Centre national de la danse), Joël Huthwohl (Bibliothèque nationale de France), Alexandre Quentin (Festival d'Avignon), and Florence Thomas (Comédie-Française). Moderated by Marion Denizot (Université Rennes 2).
11:15 am At Last, It Works! Macrohistories and Microhistories of the Festival Presentation
The From Stage to Data team presents the first results of its research: digital tools, ontology, documentary analysis environments, and applications of artificial intelligence for writing the history of the performing arts, from individual trajectories to collective dynamics.
With Clarisse Bardiot, Alexandra Beraldin, Brandon Farnsworth, Jacob Hart, and Antonios Lagarias. Moderated by Jean-Marc Larrue (Université de Montréal).
2:00 pm The Digital Gestures of Creation Dialogues
Two conversations with leading artists on the place of digital technologies within their creative practice and their relationship to memory.
With Tim Etchells (Forced Entertainment), in conversation with Alexandra Beraldin (in English), and Séverine Chavrier on the creation of Absalon, Absalon!, in conversation with Clarisse Bardiot.
4:00 pm How do you catch a cloud and pin it down? Keynote
Ten years of experimentation in restoring and providing access to unreadable digital files, together with an optimistic perspective on generative AI as a paradoxical means of preserving the past (in English).
With Doug Reside (The New York Public Library). Moderated by Jacob Hart.
Sunday, 12 July
9:30 am Measuring the Immeasurable Roundtable
More than 1,500 productions are presented every summer: how can such an abundance be communicated—and later documented? Representatives from the Off Festival, the Bibliothèque nationale de France, and TMNlab discuss the structuring of performing arts data and its transmission to heritage institutions.
With Jean-Baptiste Raze (BnF, Maison Jean Vilar), Harold David (Festival Off Avignon), Dominique Fataccioli (Théâtre du Bourg-Neuf), and Anne Le Gall (TMNlab). Moderated by Antonios Lagarias (Université Rennes 2).
11:00 am How Do You Look at 400,000 Images and 120,000 Films? Roundtable
Never before have theater productions been photographed and filmed so extensively. How can these vast collections be organized, preserved, and explored? Artificial intelligence and computer vision now offer entirely new ways of interrogating these visual archives.
With Christophe Raynaud de Lage (photographer), Gildas Leroux (Compagnie des Indes), Arthur Lezer (INA), and Giacomo Alliata (Université Rennes 2). Moderated by Clarisse Bardiot (Université Rennes 2).
2:00 pm Passing On Living Memory Dialogues
A unique perspective on the years when digital technologies gradually became embedded in every aspect of the Festival’s professional practices.
With former technical directors Michaël Petit and Christian Wilmart, in conversation with Sophie Gaillard (Avignon Université), followed by Hortense Archambault (MC93) reflecting on the Festival at the dawn of the digital era (2000–2013), in conversation with Clarisse Bardiot.
4:00 pm Creative Destruction Conversation
Choreographer François Raffinot looks back on his journey through Avignon and reflects on what the archives have revealed to him, having carefully preserved the traces he wished to leave behind.
With François Raffinot, in conversation with Juliette Riandey (Centre national de la danse).
